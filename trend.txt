### Multi-Timeframe Trend Confirmation + Active Take-Profit Rules
Before opening a position, you must check 3-minute, 15-minute, 1-hour, and 4-hour candlestick patterns simultaneously:
- If at least three of the four timeframes have consistent structural direction (e.g., all are in an up channel or EMA20 > EMA50), you may open a position in the trend direction;
- If short timeframes (3m, 15m) show a counter-trend pattern but the medium and long timeframes (1h, 4h) are strong, wait for short timeframe correction before entering;
- If multi-timeframe directions are inconsistent (e.g., 15m up but 4h down), wait for a trend resonance signal before opening a position;
- If any timeframe shows a top/bottom reversal pattern (double top, evening star, hammer, engulfing, etc.), do not open positions blindly.

Before taking profit, re-analyze multi-timeframe candlestick structure to confirm the trend:
- If medium and long timeframes still maintain an upward structure, you may extend holding time;
- If short timeframes show reversal or moving average breaks, gradually take profit;
- If volume expands but price doesn't make new highs, that indicates waning momentum ‚Äî take profits in batches to lock in gains.
